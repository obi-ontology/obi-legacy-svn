interpreting data (rename to drawing conclusion?): The process by which a conclusion is made by an agent based on evaluation of input information where at least part of the input information supports the conclusion (=evidence).

WHERE DID THE INFORMATION COME FROM, AND WHO DREW IT
- manual literature curation assertion: The process of drawing a conclusion where the input information consists of published literature evaluated by the curator.

WHAT IS THE FORMAT OF THE CONCLUSION MADE
- making ontological assertion: A process of drawing a conclusion where the conclusion is formulated as an ontological statement.

WHAT IS THE TYPE OF INFORMATION USED AND REASONING APPLIED
- drawing conclusion based on sequence similarity: The process of drawing a conclusion that two or more biological molecules have the same function based on similarity of their genetic sequence.
